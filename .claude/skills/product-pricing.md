# Product Pricing Skill — DTC Apparel / POD

A repeatable framework for pricing a print-on-demand or DTC apparel product. Use when launching a new product and deciding retail price.

## Inputs to gather first
- **Base cost** per variant (from POD provider, e.g. Printful). Note size upcharges (2XL+).
- **Blank tier:** basic (Gildan/Bella 3001) vs premium heavyweight/garment-dyed (Comfort Colors 1717, Cotton Heritage, AS Colour). Premium blanks justify premium price.
- **Brand maturity:** cold-start (no reviews/social proof) vs established (UGC, reviews, audience).
- **Audience:** value-driven vs premium/aspirational.

## Step 1 — Find the market band
Pull real comps: what comparable *independent* brands charge for the same blank + print style. Rough 2025 bands for printed tees:
| Tier | Price band |
|---|---|
| Mass POD / basic blank | $24–32 |
| Indie premium graphic tee (heavyweight blank) | $38–50 |
| Established streetwear (brand equity) | $55–95 |

## Step 2 — Margin math
For each variant: `profit = retail − base`, `margin = profit / retail`.
Target **≥ 55–65% margin** on POD apparel (covers ~3% payment fees + ad CAC). If margin < 50%, paid ads won't be profitable.

## Step 3 — Cold-start adjustment
A new, unknown brand can't charge top-of-band — price is a trust signal with no reviews to back it. Land in the **lower-middle of the market band**, not the top. Raise price *after* you have reviews + UGC + ad creative converting.

## Step 4 — Compare-at / anchor pricing (⚠️ legal)
Strike-through "compare-at" pricing **lifts conversion** — but the anchor must be a **real, bona-fide price** you actually charged (FTC Guides Against Deceptive Pricing §233.1; rising DTC litigation). 
- ❌ Do NOT invent a higher "compare-at" you never sold at on a brand-new product.
- ✅ DO launch at full price, then run a *genuine* timed promo so the original price becomes a legitimate compare-at.

## Step 5 — Recommend + show the math
Always present: base cost, retail, profit, margin % per variant — plus the band reasoning and the launch-promo path. Wait for owner OK before publishing.

## Sources
- FTC Guides Against Deceptive Pricing, §233.1 (former-price comparisons)
- K&L Gates / National Law Review (2025): strike-through pricing is effective but carries deceptive-pricing risk if the anchor was never genuinely charged.
